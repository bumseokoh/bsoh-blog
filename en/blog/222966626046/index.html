Sitting down to organize how I grew this blog so I could share it as an e-book actually helped me sort out my own thinking about blogging too. It’s been a huge help. Somewhere along the way my neighbor count passed 14,000, and 30,000 daily visitors is now the norm — on busy days it gets close to 100,000.

Growing this blog, I hit plenty of walls, agonized over them, and worked through each one. Back then I had no one to teach me about blogging, so I took a lot of detours and dealt with a lot of stress. For my academic background, I’d landed at a pretty solid place — a CJ subsidiary, a big company — and I worked hard at it, but I came to feel the company just wasn’t right for me. The blog was where I ran to, almost like an escape. Who could’ve guessed I’d end up making a living as a full-time blogger from it?

I clawed away at it because I didn’t want to have regrets, and the things I figured out along the way will just vanish someday if I don’t write them down and share them. I know a lot of you wonder whether it’s even worth keeping the blog going, and that plenty of people feel lost. I’m a lazy sort by nature, so if I don’t lean on the power of declaring something publicly, I just keep hesitating. So in the new year too, I’m going to pass on what I know and help people out.
